Direct answers to the questions we hear most from CISOs, program managers, and small business owners navigating DoD cybersecurity compliance.

CMMC 2.0 is the DoD's cybersecurity certification framework for defense contractors. Level 1 applies to contractors handling Federal Contract Information (FCI), requiring 17 basic practices with annual self-assessment. Level 2 applies to contractors handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), requiring compliance with all 110 NIST 800-171 controls and third-party assessment by a C3PAO. Your required level is determined by the DFARS clause in your contracts. Read our Complete Guide to CMMC 2.0 for details.

RMF ATO timelines typically range from 6 to 18 months depending on system complexity, existing security posture, and organizational readiness. The Bronze Shield has compressed RMF timelines to as few as 7 months through structured artifact development and proactive engagement with the Authorizing Official. The primary factors that extend timelines are documentation gaps and rework — not security implementation.

NIST 800-171 provides the security requirements for protecting CUI — the "what." CMMC adds the verification layer — the "prove it." CMMC Level 2 maps directly to the 110 controls in NIST 800-171 Rev 2, but requires assessment by a Certified Third-Party Assessment Organization (C3PAO) rather than self-attestation alone. Read our CMMC Level 1 vs Level 2 comparison for more detail.

Total CMMC Level 2 costs typically range from $75,000 to $250,000+ all-in. Costs vary significantly based on organization size, existing infrastructure, and current security maturity. We provide transparent, fixed-price proposals after a free initial consultation.
